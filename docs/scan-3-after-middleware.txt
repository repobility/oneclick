Why we stopped at 84
--------------------
Three of the four remaining findings are [AUC008] against the
capability-URL routes. The rule asks for "owner / tenant / relationship /
scope" checks on object-level endpoints — exactly the kind of check a
capability URL deliberately doesn't have, because the URL itself IS
the credential. The pattern is the same one used by Cipherlink,
VanishDrop, Yopass, PrivateBin, Firefox Send and wormhole.app.

Cipherlink (the prior Repobility showcase that used the same auth
model) hit the same ceiling at the exact same number — 84/100. The
two repos are independent confirmations that 84 is what capability-URL
costs you on Repobility's grading rubric. Reaching the A band (95+)
would require restructuring the auth model away from capability URLs
and toward an owner-bearing one, which is not the design.

The journey is honest: +16 points (68 → 84) by closing 12 of 16
findings via 6 focused commits. The 3 HIGH AUC008s are the
architectural ceiling.
